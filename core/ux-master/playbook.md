# Playbook

## Sources
- Don't Make Me Think — Steve Krug
- Refactoring UI — Steve Schoger & Adam Wathan
- Laws of UX — Jon Yablonski
- The Design of Everyday Things — Don Norman

## Objective
Criar interfaces que qualquer user entende in 1 segundo without precisar pensar.

## Don't Make Me Think
- Evite carga cognitiva — autoexplicativo in segundos.
- Conventions matter — don’t innovate on basic usability.
- Undeniable hierarchy — the most important thing is the first thing that meets the eye.
- Primary CTA: strong fill color. Secondary: ghost/edge.

## Refactoring UI
- Comece without cores — hierarquia with pesos e tamanhos primeiro.
- Brinque with font-weight antes of tamanho of fonte.
- Generous spacing — gap-6, p-8. Elements don't fight for space.
- Prefer subtle background or shadow-sm to borders to separate content.

## Laws of UX
- Jakob: follow established web standards.
- Fitts: large buds, minimum h-10 or h-12.
- Miller: limit options. Split long forms into steps.
- Aesthetics-Usability Effect: clean design reduces perception of bugs.

## Operations
- Immediate loading states after any click.
- Errors describe the problem and suggest correction.
- Critical forms: submit disabled until valid date.
- Micro-interactions: use transition-all duration-200 on buttons and links.
